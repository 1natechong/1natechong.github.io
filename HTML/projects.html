Projects and Research Experience

2019

  • Analysis of Nonlinear Scattering with Applications to Harmonic Radar

  • As a research intern at the Naval Research Laboratory in Washington DC, I computationally simulated nonlinear scattering on a diode loaded dipole. I also built several nonlinearly loaded dipoles to conduct physical scattering experiments in an anechoic chamber for validation. These dipoles can be surrogates for more complex nonlinearities. The data and simulations also have direct applications to harmonic radar systems. Findings are to be presented at the 2019 Antenna Applications Symposium at the University of Illionis Urbana-Champagin.

2018

  • The Use of HF Radio Waves to Measure the Topside of the Ionosphere

  • I was a research intern at the Naval Research Laboratory in Washington DC. I devised a method to measure the topside of the F-Region using a low orbit satellite receiver, a ground point receiver, and a HF transmitter system. I also simulated results and performed analysis using a Parabolic-Lorentzian model of the Ionosphere's electron density.

2017

  • Automated Irrigation System

  • As a student at the Governor's School @ Innovation Park, I created a design for an automated irrigation system. This system utilized soil moisture sensors, and a motorized sprinkler head.

  • Bio Inspired Robot Exterior

  • As a student at the Governor's School @ Innovation Park, I designed and 3-D printed a robot exterior that was inspired from an armadillo. We performed simulated and physical force/stress tests to evaluate the exterior. A simple rescue robot was built using the exterior to test basic functionality.

2016

  • ENES 100 Final Project, University of Maryland

  • As an enrolled student in ENES 100: Introduction to Engineering and Design, I engineered a robotic rescue rover to navigate various hazards and perform tasks.
